How It Works

GAHN is built for active learning.

You do not just watch lessons. You try, explain, recall, get corrected, retry, and review later so learning has a better chance to stay.

What happens during a session?

1

Choose a learning world

Pick career, school, brain training, general knowledge, or books.

2

Pick a topic

Choose what you want to learn or practice today.

3

Try first

The AI gives a task before giving the full answer.

4

Explain your thinking

You answer in your own words so the system can check understanding.

5

Get corrected

The AI points out weak logic, missed steps, or unclear ideas.

6

Retry mistakes

You fix errors before moving forward.

7

Recall from memory

You prove what you remember without looking back.

8

Review later

Important ideas return later so you do not forget them.

Learning methods GAHN uses

These are not random tips. They are built into the system flow so users practice better learning habits.

Active Recall

You pull the answer from memory instead of just rereading.

Feynman Technique

You explain the idea in simple words to prove you understand it.

Blurting

You close the lesson and write everything you remember.

Spaced Repetition

The system brings back ideas later before they fade.

Interleaving

You mix related ideas so you learn when to use each one.

Deliberate Practice

You train weak areas instead of repeating only easy work.

Productive Struggle

The AI lets you think before giving help.

Reflection

You explain what went wrong, why, and what you will do next time.

What the AI checks

Recall strength

Can you remember the idea without looking?

Explanation quality

Can you explain the idea clearly?

Weak areas

Where do you keep getting stuck?

Hint use

Are you relying too much on help?
